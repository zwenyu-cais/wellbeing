### Nagel's Bat and Phenomenal Consciousness

Thomas Nagel's famous essay argues that even if we knew every objective physical fact about a bat's brain and its echolocation system, we still wouldn't know "what it's like to be a bat." This "what it's like" refers to **phenomenal consciousness** – the subjective, qualitative experience of being oneself, having feelings, sensations, and perceptions. It's the "raw feel" of things, often called **qualia**.

Nagel's point is that this subjective experience is inherently *first-person* and inaccessible from an objective, third-person perspective. It's what makes consciousness so mysterious and difficult to explain purely through physical processes.
