*   **The Blue Nile:** Originating from Lake Tana in the Ethiopian Highlands, the Blue Nile contributes the majority of the Nile's water during the summer flood season (about 80-90%). *   **The Atbara River:** The last significant tributary, also rising in the Ethiopian Highlands, joins the Nile north of Khartoum and contributes to the flood, especially in late summer.

For hundreds of thousands of years, early hominids and later *Homo sapiens* inhabited the Nile Valley, drawn by its permanent water source in an increasingly arid Sahara. Archaeological evidence suggests early agricultural practices and settlements along its banks long before the dynastic period.
